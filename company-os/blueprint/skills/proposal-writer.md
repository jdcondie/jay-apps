# Proposal Writer

> **Trigger:** "Write a proposal for..." or "Build a proposal from this audit" or "Draft a proposal for [company]"
> **Purpose:** Generate a post-audit proposal from audit scorecard and discovery notes. Output a branded .docx proposal with opportunities, ROI, engagement options, and pricing.

---

## What This Skill Does

Takes an audit scorecard, discovery notes, or call transcript and produces:

1. A branded .docx proposal ready to send to the prospect
2. Opportunities ranked by ROI and implementation speed
3. Two engagement options with clear pricing
4. ROI projections using their specific numbers
5. A clear next step

---

## Input Requirements

To write a proposal, Claude needs:
- Company name, industry, size (employees + revenue)
- Discovery notes or audit scorecard (pain points, workflows, maturity rating)
- At least one quantified pain point (hours/week, cost, or revenue impact)
- Which engagement tier to recommend (assessment, sprint, or retainer)

---

## Proposal Structure

### 1. Business Snapshot

Recap what you heard on the call. Use their exact language.

> After our conversation on [date], here's what I heard:
>
> [2-3 sentences recapping their situation in their words. Reference specific pain points, frustrations, and goals they expressed. This is the trust-building moment.]

### 2. Top Opportunities

For each of the top 2-3 opportunities (ranked by ROI + speed to implement):

**Opportunity 1: [Name]**

| | |
|---|---|
| **The problem** | [Pain in their words] |
| **What it costs you** | [Hours/week, missed revenue, or hire needed] |
| **What we'd build** | [Specific solution, Level 1/2/3] |
| **Estimated ROI** | [Annual savings or revenue recovered] |
| **Timeline** | [Weeks to implement] |

**Opportunity 2: [Name]**
[Same structure]

**Opportunity 3: [Name]**
[Same structure]

### 3. Engagement Options

Always present two options. The assessment is the stepping stone. The retainer is the real offer.

**Option A: AI Assessment + Roadmap**
- **What you get:** Full audit report, Ops Canvas, Opportunity Matrix, ROI calculations, prioritized roadmap, and a live presentation of findings
- **Investment:** $5,000-$10,000 (one-time)
- **Timeline:** 2-3 weeks
- **What happens next:** Most clients who see the report ask "Can you just set this up for me?" That's when we move to implementation.

**Option B: Fractional Chief AI Officer (Retainer)**
- **What you get:** Monthly strategy sessions, process automation builds, team training, AI Operator development, competitive intelligence briefings, monthly impact reports
- **Investment:** $10,000-$15,000/month
- **Timeline:** 4-week recurring cadence (Executive Alignment, Department Activation, Implementation Sprint, Training + Reporting)
- **What happens next:** Month 1 delivers a CEO dashboard and 2-3 automated processes. ROI is typically visible inside 30 days.

*For small business prospects ($1M-$5M revenue, 1-20 employees):*

**Option A: AI Setup**
- **What you get:** Voice agent + lead follow-up automation, fully configured and running
- **Investment:** $2,500 (one-time setup)
- **Monthly:** $250/month for maintenance, monitoring, and support
- **Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

### 4. Pricing

Present clean. No hedging. The ROI section above should make the price feel small.

### 5. Next Step

> If this feels right, I'll send a simple agreement and we can get started [specific timeframe].
>
> Want to talk it through first? I have time on [specific day]. Let me know.

## Formatting Rules

**Brand system:**
- Navy (#1B2A4A) for headers and table header rows
- Orange (#E8541A) for accent lines, highlights, and CTAs
- Light (#F5F0EB) for alternating table rows
- Plus Jakarta Sans for web, Arial for Word documents
- Headlines: Bold/800 weight
- Body: 400 weight, 11pt

**Document standards:**
- Output as .docx
- Bracketed [fill-in fields] for anything prospect-specific
- Orange divider lines between major sections
- Tables with navy header row (white text) and light alternating rows
- Keep it to 2-3 pages max. Proposals that are too long don't get read.

## Rules

- Use their exact language from the call. Their words in your proposal = trust.
- Present ROI first, pricing second. The number should feel small by the time they see it.
- Always offer two options. Assessment as stepping stone, retainer as real offer.
- Keep it to 2-3 pages. Nobody reads a 10-page proposal for a $10K engagement.
- End with a specific next step and specific day. Not "let me know."
- Follow Jay's voice: direct, no fluff, no jargon, short paragraphs, contractions, specific numbers.
- No em dashes. No banned words.
