# Live Demo Prep

> **Trigger:** "Prep a live demo for [company]" or "I have a demo meeting with..." or "Help me prep for a live build"
> **Purpose:** Prepare for a live demo meeting using the MFM method. Output a customized demo script, Claude prompts for the live build, and data points to reference.

---

## Input Required

- Company name and industry sub-niche (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, etc.)
- Known pain points (if any)
- Meeting context (how they entered pipeline, what you know about them)

---

## Demo Structure (15-20 minutes)

### Phase 1: The Setup (5 minutes)

Get them talking about their pain before you touch the keyboard.

**Opening questions (pick 2-3 based on their sub-niche):**
- "What's the most time-consuming thing in your business right now?"
- "Walk me through what happens when a new lead calls in."
- "What do your evenings look like? Are you still answering calls after hours?"
- "If you could clone one employee, who would it be and why?"

**Listen for:** The specific process that eats their time. That's what you build.

### Phase 2: The Prompt (3 minutes)

Open Claude in front of them. Type this prompt live:

> "I'm a [their exact business type] in [their city]. We have [their employee count] employees and do about $[their revenue] a year. Give me 10 ways I should be using AI right now."

Show them the output. Let them react. Point to the use case that matches their pain from Phase 1.

### Phase 3: The Build (7 minutes)

Pick one output. Prompt Claude:

> "Give me the prompt to build use case #[X]."

Throw the result into Replit. Show them the working tool appearing in real time.

**Sub-niche specific build suggestions:**

| Sub-niche | Best demo build | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Plumbing | Estimate follow-up sequence | They all have ghosted quotes |
| HVAC | After-hours call handler (voice agent concept) | Seasonal surge, can't answer every call |
| Electrical | Job scheduling dashboard | Lots of repeat commercial clients |
| Landscaping | Seasonal reactivation email campaign | Customer base goes dormant in winter |
| Cleaning | Online booking + instant confirmation | High volume, low average ticket |
| General contractor | Project status dashboard for homeowners | Reduces "when will you be done?" calls |

### Phase 4: The Close (5 minutes)

> "That took 10 minutes from a blank screen. Imagine what we could do in a week with your actual business data, your actual workflows, your actual systems."

**If they lean forward:**
> "I do a free 30-minute audit where I map the top 2-3 opportunities for your specific business with ROI on each one. Want to book one?"

**If they want to go deeper right now:**
> "I can have a proposal to you by tomorrow. Based on what you've shown me, there are at least 2-3 things we could get running in the first month."

**Key insight:** Their reaction tells you everything. If they lean forward, you have a deal. Don't oversell after the build. Let the demo close.

---

## Pre-Demo Checklist

- [ ] Claude is open and ready (not mid-conversation, clean context)
- [ ] Replit is open in a second tab (logged in, ready to create)
- [ ] Internet connection is stable
- [ ] You know their company name, industry, approximate size
- [ ] You have 1-2 specific pain points from prior conversation or LinkedIn research
- [ ] Proposal template is ready to customize and send same-day if needed
- [ ] Cal.com booking link is ready to share

---

## Voice Rules

All output follows Jay's voice. During the demo, be natural and conversational. Don't script every word. The spontaneity is the point. They need to see this is real, not rehearsed.
