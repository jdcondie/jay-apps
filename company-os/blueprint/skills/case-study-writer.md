# Case Study Writer

> **Trigger:** "Write a case study for [client]" or "Build a case study from this engagement"
> **Purpose:** Write a case study from engagement data. Output a formatted case study with problem/solution/results structure.

---

## Input Required

- Client name (or anonymized descriptor like "a $3M plumbing company")
- Industry, location, revenue, team size
- Problems identified (from audit or discovery)
- Solutions implemented (with solution levels)
- Results (hours saved, costs reduced, revenue recovered, hires avoided)
- Timeline (how long to implement)
- Client quote (optional but preferred)

---

## Reference: Summit Plumbing Projected Case Study

Use this as a template for real case studies once clients are engaged:

**Company:** Summit Plumbing & Drain, $3.2M revenue, 18 employees, Salt Lake City
**Problems:** 35-40% calls outside hours (missed leads), slow response time (>30 min avg), no estimate follow-up system, no centralized lead tracking
**Solutions:** Voice agent (24/7), automated lead response (<5 min), estimate follow-up sequence (3-touch), CRM integration
**Projected recovery:** $8,400/month ($4,200 missed calls + $2,100 faster response + $2,100 estimate follow-up)

---

## Formatting

- Output as .md for internal use or .docx for client-facing/marketing use
- Navy/orange brand system for .docx
- Keep it to 1-2 pages. Case studies that are too long don't get read.
- Lead with the result in the headline. Make the number the hook.

---

## Rules

- Only write case studies from real engagement data. Don't fabricate results.
- The Summit Plumbing case is a projected/modeled example. Label it clearly as "projected" until replaced with actual numbers.
- Use specific numbers, not ranges. "$8,400/month" not "thousands per month."
- Include the timeline. Speed of implementation is a selling point.
- Jay's voice: direct, short, specific. No em dashes, no banned words.
