# Processes

## Sales Process

### Entry Paths

Three paths into the pipeline. All funnel to a free AI efficiency audit.

| Starting Position | Best Path | First Move |
|---|---|---|
| No network, no warm leads | Seminar (Chamber of Commerce) | Book a date, prepare a 45-min AI tutorial |
| 1 warm business relationship | Live demo (MFM method) | Get a meeting, run the demo in the room |
| Already in a niche (marketing, ops, etc.) | Niche audit + template | Build one use case for one client, replicate |
| Technical background | Mid-market vibe-coded apps | Target 10-50 person companies, $15K setup |

### The Conversion Ladder

Seminar/outreach -> Free audit -> Assessment ($5-15K) -> Sprint ($5-15K) -> Retainer ($10-25K/mo) -> Dev partner commissions ($2-10K/referral).

A Chamber of Commerce seminar lead books a free discovery call. You sell the $10K audit. The audit surfaces 3 Quick Wins and 2 Big Swings. You sell a $10K implementation sprint for the Quick Wins. The sprint delivers visible ROI. You propose the $15K/month retainer to tackle the Big Swings and ongoing optimization. Total first-year value from one lead: $190K+.

For small business clients, the path is simpler: demo -> setup ($2,500) -> monthly retainer ($250).

## Discovery Call Framework

**Cadence:** As booked (target: 2-3 per week during launch)
**Owner:** Jay
**Tools:** Google Calendar (scheduling), Granola/Fireflies.ai (transcript), Notion (notes)

### The Rule

**First call is discovery only. No solutioning.** As soon as you start prescribing, discovery stops. The goal is to surface monetizable problems, not demonstrate your knowledge.

If pressed for a solution, use this script:

> "That's a great question. There are 2 or 3 tools that could handle that, but I don't want to give you a generic answer. I want to go away, test them against your specific workflow, and come back with the right one in the report. Sound good?"

### STE Framework Call Structure

#### Opening (5 minutes)

Set a low-pressure tone. Frame it as a conversation, not a sales call.

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making the time. This is mostly just a discovery conversation. I want to understand where you guys are at with AI before I say anything about what we do. Sound good?"

Keep it short. Get them talking within 60 seconds. If they ask "So what do you do?" deflect briefly: "I'll get to that in a second. I want to make sure it's even relevant to you first."

#### Step 1: Strategy — Speak to the Fear (10 minutes)

Every CEO at a $10M-$50M company is quietly worried they're falling behind on AI. Open that door.

**Ask:** "Are you guys using AI at all right now?"

**What they'll say:** "A couple of us use ChatGPT for emails." (Almost every time.)

**Your response:**
> "Honestly, that's exactly where most companies are. The fact that you're even on this call puts you ahead of a lot of people. What we do is come in and help you understand how AI is going to affect your specific industry, what your competitors are probably doing right now, and where the opportunities are before they get obvious."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Have you had any internal conversations about AI, like, what you should actually be doing with it?"
- "Is there pressure from ownership or leadership to figure this out?"
- "What's your gut feeling, do you think you're ahead or behind on this?"

**Listen for:** Any language around competition, falling behind, not knowing where to start.

#### Step 2: Transformation — Show the ROI (15 minutes)

Tie your cost to a specific savings or revenue gain. Make the math obvious.

**Ask:** "Let me ask you something. Is there anyone you've been wanting to hire? A role you need filled, or a task that keeps falling through the cracks?"

**What they'll say:** Something like "We really need a marketing coordinator" or "We need someone handling follow-ups" or "We're drowning in admin."

**Your response:**
> "Okay. That person was probably going to cost you $6,000 to $8,000 a month fully loaded. What if instead we set up something that costs $25 a month to run, works around the clock, and handles that automatically? That's usually where we start."

**Then layer in additional wins:**
- "We'd also build out a CEO dashboard. One place where you can see your whole business instead of logging into five different tools."
- "We typically automate 3 to 5 of the most manual processes in the first 60 days. Things like CRM data entry, lead follow-up, scheduling, reporting."

**Key question:**
> "If you stopped needing to hire that person, and you got those processes running automatically, what would that mean for you personally? Your time? Your margins?"

**Listen for:** Dollar figures, time savings, specific frustrations. Reflect them back exactly.

#### Step 3: Education — Expand the Value (5-10 minutes)

Show that the impact goes beyond operations.

> "Here's the thing. We've already talked about saving you a hire and automating your core processes. But what if we could also get 15 to 20 percent more productivity out of the team you already have? The people already on payroll? On a company your size, that's not a small number."

**If they ask how:**
> "We run regular training sessions with your team. Practical stuff, not theory. How to use AI in their specific roles, how to build simple tools themselves. The goal is to make every employee more capable, not just give you a shiny dashboard."

**Anchor the value:**
> "So we're talking about: staying ahead of your competitors, avoiding 1 to 2 hires, automating your core processes, and upgrading your entire team. That's the full picture."

#### The Close

After STE, the price should feel small against the value you've outlined.

> "Based on what you've shared, I think there's a strong fit here. The way we work is as your fractional Chief AI Officer. We come in on a monthly basis, handle the strategy, build out the automations, and train your team. Depending on the size and scope, that's typically $10,000 to $15,000 a month."

**Then stop talking.** Let them respond.

**If they hesitate on price:**
> "Think about it this way. If we help you avoid one $8K hire and add 15% productivity to a team of 20, that's a pretty fast payback. Most clients see positive ROI inside the first 30 days."

**If they're not ready for a retainer:**
> "Totally fine. A lot of clients start with a one-time AI assessment and roadmap. That's $5,000 to $10,000, gives you a clear picture of where to go, and usually opens up 3 to 5 specific opportunities we can start executing on. Want to start there?"

#### Closing the Call

Always end with a specific next step:
> "Here's what I'll do. I'll put together a short recap of what we talked about and come back to you with 2 or 3 specific ways we'd approach this for [their company/industry]. Does [specific day] work to reconnect?"

- Never leave without a next step on the calendar.
- Send a follow-up within 24 hours: 3-5 sentences recapping their pain in their words, plus the 2-3 opportunity areas.

## Discovery Question Bank

Use these in order. Not all questions in every meeting. Read the room and follow the thread.

### Part 1: AI Sentiment (Ask First, Always)

Establish where they are before you say anything. Never correct. Never educate yet.

- "Where are you personally with AI right now?"
- "What excites you about it, if anything?"
- "What worries you about it?"
- "What have you already tried, tools, experiments, anything?"
- "Has there been any internal pressure to figure this out?"

**Listen for:** Fear of falling behind, scattered usage, zero strategy, leadership pressure.

### Part 2: Workflow Mapping (The Core Discovery)

Map real work, not tools. Follow the thread with "what happens next?" until you hit friction.

**Opening question:**
> "Walk me through a typical week. Not the big picture stuff, but the actual work. What are people doing, what are you doing, where does time go?"

**Follow-up drill:**
- "What happens next after that?"
- "Who handles that step?"
- "How long does that usually take?"
- "How often does that happen, daily, weekly?"
- "Where does it break down or slow down?"
- "Is there a person whose whole job is basically just that one thing?"

**Pattern questions (after they've talked for a while):**
- "What do you do 10 to 20 times a week that's repetitive and information-dependent?"
- "Where does your team spend time on things that follow a predictable pattern?"
- "Where do you lose leads or customers because of slow response time?"
- "What data do you have sitting somewhere that nobody's doing anything with?"

**Listen for:** Manual steps, handoffs, repetition, judgment calls that follow a pattern, bottlenecks tied to one person.

### Part 3: Pain Quantification

Turn vague frustration into a number. This is what makes the ROI case.

- "How many hours a week does that take, roughly?"
- "How many people are involved in that process?"
- "What does it cost you when that breaks down or slows down?"
- "Is there a hire you've been wanting to make because of this problem?"
- "What would change if this ran automatically, for you, for your team?"
- "What happens if nothing changes here in the next 12 months?"

**Rule:** Get at least one dollar figure or time figure attached to each pain point. Without a number, you can't make the ROI case on the close.

### Part 4: Use Case Matching

Once you've heard the pain, mentally map it to the universal use cases.

**Voice / Phone:**
- "How many inbound calls do you get per day?"
- "What percentage of those are repetitive, appointments, FAQs, basic questions?"
- "What happens when a call comes in after hours or during a busy period?"

**Lead / Data Tracking:**
- "How are you currently tracking leads or prospects?"
- "Is there a manual step where someone has to log or update something?"
- "Are there public signals, reviews, news, job postings, that would tell you something useful about a prospect if someone was monitoring them?"

**Contract / Document Monitoring:**
- "How many active contracts, leases, or agreements are you managing at any one time?"
- "How do you track expiration dates or renewal windows right now?"
- "Has anything ever slipped through because nobody caught a deadline?"

**Communication / Team Coaching:**
- "Is there a lot of internal communication, Slack, email, text?"
- "Do you have situations where tone or approach in a message caused a problem?"
- "Do managers spend time coaching people on how to communicate with clients or each other?"

**Custom CRM / Ops Tool:**
- "What tools are you currently paying for to run your operations?"
- "What are you paying monthly for your CRM or project management tools?"
- "Is there a tool you're paying for that nobody really uses correctly?"

### Part 5: Niche Signal Questions

Use these to decide if this industry could become your niche.

- "Is this a problem every company in your space has, or is it specific to you?"
- "When you talk to other [industry] owners, is this something that comes up?"
- "Do you know other owners who would benefit from what we've been talking about?"

**Listen for:** "Oh yeah, everyone in our industry has this problem." That's niche confirmation. Build once, deploy many times.

### Part 6: Closing Discovery

End every discovery session with these. They surface value in their words, which you reflect back in your proposal.

- "Of everything we've talked about, what's the one thing that would make the biggest difference if it was just solved?"
- "What would change for you personally if the operational stuff ran more automatically?"
- "What would you need to see to feel confident this is worth moving forward?"

### Use Case Trigger Map

| If they say... | Likely use case | Next question |
|---|---|---|
| "We miss calls all the time" | Voice agent | "What do most of those calls need, an appointment, info, or something else?" |
| "We need to hire someone for follow-up" | Automation + CRM | "Walk me through what that person would actually do every day." |
| "We have a ton of data but don't use it" | Dashboard / reporting | "What decisions would you make differently if you could see that data easily?" |
| "Our team is inconsistent with clients" | Communication coach | "Is it tone, timing, or content that's the problem?" |
| "We're paying for tools we don't use right" | Custom CRM | "What does your current stack cost you monthly?" |
| "We manage a lot of contracts/leases" | Document monitor | "What's your current process for tracking renewals?" |

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We're too small for this" | "That's where we see biggest impact. No CTO. Nobody's evaluated this. You're exactly the profile where AI makes the most difference." |
| "We tried ChatGPT, didn't work" | "That's Level 0. Using ChatGPT manually, one question at a time, is a start. We build systems that run without anyone having to remember to prompt it." |
| "Can't we just hire someone?" | "One person costs $6K/mo. AI costs $25/mo, works 24/7, never quits. Most of what you'd hire for can be automated at a fraction of the cost." |
| "We have an IT person for this" | "IT and AI strategy are different problems. Most IT people are keeping the lights on. They're not building AI workflows or training teams. Different gap." |
| "Can you send me some info first?" | "Sure, but honestly the most useful thing is a 20-minute call where I can ask you a few specific questions. Info without context doesn't mean much. Can we do [day]?" |
| "We tried AI tools before, didn't work" | "That's common. Most implementations fail because someone bought a tool without mapping the workflow first. We do it the other way around. Workflow first, tools second." |
| "We don't have budget right now" | "Understood. What if I showed you where the budget already exists, just going to the wrong places? That's usually what we find in the first 30 days." |

## Audit / Assessment Process

### AI Maturity Rating

Score each area 1-5. Total out of 30.

| Area | 1 (Low) | 5 (High) |
|---|---|---|
| Tool awareness | Never heard of AI tools | Knows multiple platforms and use cases |
| Current usage | Zero AI usage | AI integrated into daily workflows |
| Data readiness | No organized data | Clean, accessible, structured data |
| Process documentation | No documented processes | SOPs for all core workflows |
| Team openness | Resistant to change | Eager to learn and adopt |
| Leadership urgency | No interest | Active priority from CEO |

**Maturity Bands:**
- 6-12: AI-Exposed (aware it exists, not using it)
- 13-18: AI-Behind (some usage, no strategy)
- 19-24: AI-Aware (engaged, needs structure)
- 25-30: AI-Ready (aligned, ready to implement)

### Workflow Discovery Template

For each of the top 3 workflows surfaced in discovery:

| Field | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Workflow name | Short description |
| Steps involved | Step-by-step current process |
| Who touches it | Roles/people involved |
| Frequency | Daily, weekly, monthly |
| Time per cycle | Hours per occurrence |
| AI opportunity level | Level 1, 2, or 3 |
| Estimated value | Time saved x hourly rate = annual savings |

### ROI Quantification Method

**Three questions to ask every time a painful task surfaces:**
1. "Roughly how many hours a week do you spend on that?"
2. "Who is doing that task? Is it you, or a junior admin?"
3. "Ballpark, what's the hourly cost for that role? Are we talking $20/hr or $100/hr?"

**Direct cost savings formula:**
Hours saved/week x hourly rate = weekly savings. Weekly savings x 52 = annual savings. Annual savings / implementation cost = ROI multiple.

**Revenue uplift formula (use conservatively):**
Estimate what percentage of saved time can be reallocated to revenue-generating work (use 50% as conservative baseline). Multiply reallocated hours by revenue value of that activity.

Present both numbers. Cost savings are the floor. Revenue uplift is the ceiling. The retainer price should sit well below the floor.

### Solution Brief Template

Every opportunity surfaced in discovery gets captured in a Solution Brief before any work begins.

| Field | What to Fill In |
|---|---|
| Problem | One sentence description of the pain |
| Current workflow | Step-by-step how it works today |
| Time and cost today | Hours per week, people involved, dollar estimate |
| Stakeholders | Who owns this process |
| Solution level | 1, 2, or 3 |
| Estimated ROI | Time saved, hire avoided, revenue recovered |

### The Ops Canvas and Opportunity Matrix

**Step 1: Build the Ops Canvas.** Map the client's core operations into three engines: Acquisition (how they find and sign customers), Delivery (how they deliver their product or service), and Support (how they handle post-sale issues). Tag steps that are time sinks or quality risks.

**Step 2: Build the Opportunity Matrix.** Plot tagged items on a 2x2 grid. X-axis = business impact. Y-axis = implementation effort.

- **Quick Wins (low effort, high impact):** Priority #1. Start here always. Earns trust, justifies retainer.
- **Big Swings (high effort, high impact):** Retainer roadmap. "Once Quick Wins are in place, these are next."
- **Nice-to-Haves (low effort, low impact):** Extra value, never the focus.
- **Deprioritize (high effort, low impact):** Actively avoid. Knowing what not to build is part of the value.

**Step 3: Validate with the client.** Review the matrix with the stakeholder before finalizing. Co-creation builds trust and means you're presenting a plan you've already agreed on.

## Post-Audit Proposal Delivery

**Cadence:** Within 48 hours of discovery call
**Owner:** Jay
**Tools:** Claude Code (proposal generation), Word/docx (client-facing document)

### Proposal Structure

1. **Business snapshot:** Recap what you heard in their words
2. **Top 2-3 opportunities:** For each: pain, solution, estimated ROI
3. **Engagement options:**
   - Option A: AI Assessment + Roadmap ($5,000-$10,000 one-time)
   - Option B: Fractional CAIO Retainer ($10,000-$15,000/month)
4. **Pricing:** Clean, no hedging
5. **Next step:** "If this feels right, I'll send a simple agreement"

**Key principle:** Use their exact language from the call. When they see their own words in the proposal, trust compounds.

**Format:** Branded .docx, navy/orange, bracketed [fill-in fields] for company-specific details.

## 4-Week Retainer Cadence (The AI Operating System)

**Cadence:** Recurring monthly cycle for active retainer clients
**Owner:** Jay
**Tools:** Claude Code (builds), Notion (tracking), Google Calendar (sessions), Granola (transcripts)

### Week 1: Executive Alignment
- CEO strategy session
- KPI review and roadmap refresh
- Competitive intelligence briefing
- Priority setting for the month

### Week 2: Department Activation
- Embed with team, map workflows
- Surface new AI opportunities
- Run end-user interviews (3-5 at 30-45 min each for larger orgs)
- Capture Solution Briefs for new opportunities

### Week 3: Implementation Sprint
- Build and deploy new automations
- Test and iterate on existing systems
- Quick Wins from the Opportunity Matrix first

### Week 4: Training + Reporting
- Team training sessions (practical, role-specific)
- Monthly impact report delivery
- Updated roadmap presentation
- AI Operator development check-in

**Output:** Monthly impact report (.docx), updated roadmap, trained AI Operator inside client's org.

## Rules for All Calls

- Never solution before discovery is done. As soon as you pitch, they stop talking.
- Repeat their words back to them. It signals you're listening and builds trust.
- Silence is fine. Ask a question, then wait. Don't fill the gap.
- On referral calls, close faster. They already want it. Just make it easy to say yes.
- Record every call (Fireflies.ai or Google Meet recording). Listen, don't take notes.
- After the call, feed the transcript to Claude: "Analyze this business, identify friction points, quantify impact, recommend investigation areas."
- The goal is 2-3 use cases, not 10. Specific beats comprehensive.
- Ask "what happens next?" at least 5 times. The real pain is usually 3-4 steps deep.
- Every call ends with a scheduled next step. No "I'll follow up sometime."
