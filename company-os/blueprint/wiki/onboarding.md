# Client Onboarding

## Working Principles

Six rules that define how we work with every client:

1. **Build before you explain.** A working demo within the first 2 weeks beats any presentation. Show, don't tell.
2. **You own what we build.** Everything lives in your systems. No lock-in. If we part ways, you keep everything.
3. **I teach, not just deliver.** Your team gets more capable over time. The goal is making you better, not dependent.
4. **One clear priority at a time.** We maintain a backlog, but we focus on one sprint item. Scattered effort kills results.
5. **Multiple channels to reach you.** Email for formal updates, text for quick questions, scheduled calls for strategy, Loom videos for walkthroughs.
6. **What we need from you:** Access to systems, timely decisions, time for team interviews. The faster you respond, the faster we deliver.

## Pre-Audit Prep (What to Expect)

Sent to prospects before the audit call.

### What the audit covers

1. **AI Maturity Assessment:** Where your company sits on a 1-30 scale across 6 dimensions
2. **Workflow Discovery:** Your top 3 most time-consuming, repetitive processes mapped step-by-step
3. **Gap Identification:** Where you're losing time, money, or leads right now
4. **ROI Projection:** Dollar value of fixing the top 2-3 gaps
5. **Recommended Next Steps:** Specific use cases with solution level, timeline, and investment

### What to have ready

- How many employees you have and what roles
- What tools/software you currently pay for
- Your biggest operational bottleneck (the thing that eats the most time)
- Any roles you've been wanting to hire for
- What happens when a call comes in after business hours

### What happens after

- Scorecard delivered within 24 hours
- If there's a fit, we'll present 2-3 specific opportunities with ROI on a follow-up call
- No obligation. The audit is valuable regardless of whether we work together.

- [ ] Kick-off call with owner/CEO (30 min)
- [ ] Set communication norms (email for updates, text for quick questions, scheduled calls for strategy)
- [ ] Access granted: CRM login, calendar system, Google Drive folder, any existing SOPs
- [ ] Collect: current tech stack list, employee roster, org chart, sample workflows
- [ ] Review the AI Operating System framework (4-week retainer cadence)
- [ ] Identify internal AI Operator candidate (curious, high-agency employee)
- [ ] Set expectations: first deliverable (CEO dashboard or voice agent) within 2 weeks

## Week 1: AI Audit + Quick Wins

- [ ] Complete AI efficiency audit across all departments (Maturity Scorecard)
- [ ] Run stakeholder interview with CEO (30,000-foot view)
- [ ] Run 3-5 end-user interviews at 30-45 min each (ground-level reality)
- [ ] Identify top 3 Quick Wins (prioritize by ROI and implementation speed)
- [ ] Begin Level 1 implementation (native AI tools for immediate time savings)
- [ ] Deliver audit findings document with Ops Canvas, Opportunity Matrix, and ROI projections
- [ ] Schedule Week 2 department activation sessions

## Week 2-4: Build + Train

- [ ] Build CEO dashboard (connect to existing systems, interactive, AI-queryable)
- [ ] Deploy first automation (voice agent, lead follow-up, or estimate follow-up)
- [ ] Automate 2-3 additional manual processes from the Quick Wins list
- [ ] Run department-specific AI training sessions (practical, role-specific)
- [ ] Train AI Operator on discovery conversations and Solution Brief format
- [ ] Deliver first monthly impact report with baseline metrics
- [ ] Set 90-day roadmap with priorities for months 2-3
- [ ] Document before/after for each automation (hours saved, steps removed)

## AI Operator Development

The AI Operator is the internal champion who owns AI adoption inside the client's org. Typically a curious, high-agency employee with strong communication skills. Not necessarily technical.

### Three roles of the AI Operator

1. **Discovery:** Find problems worth solving. Talk to teammates, listen for repetitive tasks, surface opportunities.
2. **Translation:** Turn messy complaints into structured Solution Briefs (Problem, Current Workflow, Time/Cost, Stakeholders, Solution Level, Estimated ROI).
3. **Enablement:** Help teammates adopt tools, reduce friction, celebrate wins.

### Discovery questions for the AI Operator

- "What do you do every week that follows the same pattern?"
- "Where do you spend time that feels automatic?"
- "What takes longest between step A and step B?"
- "What information do you wish you had faster?"
- "Is there something you do that you think a computer could probably do?"

### Development timeline

- **Month 1:** Shadow Jay on interviews and builds. Learn the Solution Brief format.
- **Month 2:** Run discovery conversations independently. Submit briefs for review.
- **Month 3:** Evaluate Level 1/2/3 independently. Reduce reliance on Jay for tactical execution.

**Why this matters:** A coach who trained their team is much harder to fire than a consultant who just built a tool. The goal is embedded methodology, not just deliverables.
